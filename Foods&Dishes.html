Foods and Dishes

Exquisite Filipino Cuisine brimming with Love

Filipino foods are quite impressive, especially when the elderly cook for us; it's like tasting a magical recipe. Check out these foods that will make you drool. To see is to believe, really!

all time favorites

Main Dish

Adobo is popular among filipinos for its mixture of sweet and salty flavors. Because of its popularity, many filipinos considered it as the national dish of the Philippines.

Appetizer

Lumpiang Shanghai is our very own Filipino version of spring rolls. It is loved by many, both Filipinos and foreigners, due to its crispy exterior and tasty filling

Soup

Sinigang na hipon is a Filipino shrimp soup characterized by its sour and strongly savory flavor. It is considered a homemade specialty here in the Philippines

Beverages

Sago’t Gulaman is sweet with brown sugar and water.It’s sold in most of the local markets. It’s one of the most popular Filipino drinks.

Meryenda

Banana-cue, like barbecue, is skewered on a stick, and cooked in brown sugar until a crisp, caramelized crust forms around it.

Typical Breakfast

Champorado is a Filipino chocolate rice porridge that is traditionally served for breakfast. It’s typically served with tuyo (dried salted fish).

our food gallery

Taho

Taho is a sweet Filipino dessert which consists of fresh soft tofu doused in arnibal syrup and sprinkled with plump sago pearls.

Bilo-Bilo

is a popular Filipino treat made with chewy rice balls cooked in a creamy coconut milk blend.

Afritada

Afritada is one of many traditional Filipino stews which use a tangy tomato sauce as the key ingredient.

Tokneneng

Tokneneng is a traditional Filipino dish that's sold as a street food snack. It consists of hard-boiled eggs that are dipped in an orange-colored batter, then deep-fried.

Adobong Pusit

Adobong pusit is a traditional Filipino dish and is prepared with a combination of fresh squid and its ink, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, salt, sugar, oil, onions, and tomatoes

Bistek Tagalog

Bistek Tagalog is a traditional meat dish from the Philippines. It's made with thinly sliced beef marinated and braised in citrus juice , onions, garlic, soy sauce, and pepper.

Arroz Caldo

While chicken soup is popular in the West, Filipinos prefer arroz caldo, a thick chicken rice porridge.

Leche flan

Leche flan is a Filipino dessert that is essentially a caramel custard consisting of milk, sugar, and eggs, with the addition of vanilla flavoring.

Lechon

Lechon, derived from a Spanish word for roasted suckling pig is one of the most popular dishes in the Philippines. The slowly-roasted suckling pig is usually stuffed with lemongrass, tamarind, garlic, onions, and chives, and is then roasted on a large bamboo spit over an open fire. It is traditionally served whole on a platter, at celebrations and festive events such as weddings and Christmas.
